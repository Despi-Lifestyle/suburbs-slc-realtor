By Suburb

What's at your doorstep

Each suburb has different strengths for outdoor recreation. Here's what you get from each location.

Cottonwood Heights
The undisputed outdoor champion
SkiingHikingMountain BikingParks

If outdoor access is your #1 priority, it's hard to beat Cottonwood Heights. You're literally at the mouth of Big Cottonwood Canyon, with Brighton and Solitude ski resorts a 20-minute drive up the canyon. Hiking trails start right from the neighborhood. The Bonneville Shoreline Trail is practically in your backyard.

🎿 Ski resorts: 20-25 min
🥾 Trailheads: 5 min
🚴 Bike trails: 5 min
Draper
Trail running and mountain biking mecca
HikingMountain BikingSkiingParks

Corner Canyon is the crown jewel — over 40 miles of trails for hiking, mountain biking, and trail running right from the neighborhood. The BST (Bonneville Shoreline Trail) connects here too. Ski resorts are 30-35 minutes via the canyons.

🎿 Ski resorts: 30-35 min
🥾 Corner Canyon: 5 min
🚴 40+ miles of trails: At doorstep
Sandy
Canyon access with urban amenities
SkiingHikingParksWater Sports

Sandy sits right at the mouth of Little Cottonwood Canyon — Snowbird and Alta are a quick drive up. Bell Canyon and Corner Canyon trails are close, plus the city has excellent parks and rec facilities. The Jordan River Parkway runs through for easy cycling and running.

🎿 Snowbird/Alta: 25-30 min
🥾 Bell Canyon: 10 min
🌊 Jordan River: 10 min
South Jordan
Balanced access to everything
ParksCyclingDaybreak LakeSkiing

South Jordan's Daybreak community features its own lake, miles of paved trails, and community pools. The Oquirrh Mountain range provides a dramatic western backdrop. Ski resorts are 35-40 minutes east. Great for families who want accessible everyday recreation.

🎿 Ski resorts: 35-40 min
🏖️ Daybreak Lake: In community
🚴 Paved trails: At doorstep
Herriman, Riverton, Bluffdale
Wide open spaces and growing trail networks
HikingCyclingOpen Space

These southwest suburbs offer a different outdoor vibe — more wide-open space, foothills, and equestrian trails. Butterfield Canyon, Rose Canyon, and Yellow Fork trails provide excellent hiking. Ski resorts are a bit farther (40-50 min) but the trade-off is larger lots and a more rural feel with growing amenities.

🎿 Ski resorts: 40-50 min
🥾 Foothill trails: 10-15 min
🐴 Equestrian: Available
Holladay & Millcreek
Central location, canyon proximity
HikingSkiingCyclingCity Parks

Both suburbs sit close to Millcreek Canyon — a popular year-round destination for hiking, mountain biking (odd days), and cross-country skiing. Big and Little Cottonwood Canyons are also easily accessible. Urban parks like Holladay Lions Park add everyday green space.

🎿 Ski resorts: 25-35 min
🥾 Millcreek Canyon: 10-15 min
🌳 City parks: Walking distance
