SLC Suburbs School District Overview

A quick breakdown of the districts serving Salt Lake City's suburbs — ratings, enrollment, and what parents need to know.

Quick Facts

What Utah schools get right

Utah's education system has unique advantages that surprise many relocating families.

High Graduation Rates

Utah's suburban school districts consistently post graduation rates above 90%, well above the national average.

Smaller Class Sizes

Many suburban schools maintain lower student-to-teacher ratios than urban counterparts, especially in newer schools.

STEM Focus

Utah has invested heavily in STEM education. Many suburbs have dedicated STEM academies and advanced programs.

District Profiles

The districts serving SLC suburbs

Four main school districts serve the nine suburbs in this guide. Here's what you need to know about each.

Canyons School District

★ 8-9/10 Rating
Students: 33,000+
Schools: 50+
Founded: 2009

One of Utah's top-performing districts. Canyons is known for strong academic programs, excellent STEM offerings, and some of the best high schools in the state. Corner Canyon High School and Brighton High School are standout campuses. The district is newer (established 2009) which means modern facilities and innovative programs.

Draper Sandy (East) Cottonwood Heights Holladay Millcreek (parts)

Jordan School District

★ 7-9/10 Rating
Students: 55,000+
Schools: 70+
Established: Long-standing

The largest district in the south valley, Jordan SD is well-established with a wide range of programs. It offers dual-language immersion, IB programs, and strong extracurricular activities. Performance varies by school, but the best schools here compete with any in the state. South Jordan and Riverton schools particularly shine.

South Jordan Riverton Herriman Bluffdale Sandy (West)

Granite School District

★ 6-8/10 Rating
Students: 60,000+
Schools: 80+
Diversity: Most diverse in Utah

Utah's most diverse school district, Granite serves a wide socioeconomic range. While overall ratings are slightly lower, individual schools in the Millcreek and Cottonwood Heights areas rate quite well. The district offers extensive language programs and strong special education services.

Millcreek (parts) Cottonwood Heights (parts) Holladay (parts)

Natalie's Note for Parents

School boundaries in the SLC suburbs can be tricky — a home on one side of a street might feed into a different school than the house across from it. Don't assume based on city alone. I always verify exact school assignments for every home I show to families. If schools are a top priority for your family (and they usually are), let me know and I'll make sure we're only looking at homes in the right boundaries.
