Research & Planning

12–8 weeks before your move

Define your family's priorities

Rank what matters most — commute distance, school ratings, lot size, mountain access, budget. SLC suburbs vary widely: Draper and Cottonwood Heights are closest to canyons, Herriman and Riverton offer more space for the money.

Research the 9 SLC suburbs

Each suburb has a distinct personality, price range, and lifestyle. Explore South Jordan, Draper, Herriman, Sandy, Cottonwood Heights, Holladay, Millcreek, Riverton, and Bluffdale.

Get your finances in order

Pull credit reports, pay down debts, and avoid opening new credit accounts. Start saving for your down payment and closing costs (typically 2–5% of purchase price in Utah).

Get pre-approved for a mortgage

Compare rates from 2–3 lenders. In Utah's market, sellers take pre-approval letters seriously. Ask about Utah Housing Corporation (UHC) programs if you're a first-time buyer.

Research school districts (if you have kids)

Canyons District serves Draper, Sandy, Cottonwood Heights, and Holladay. Jordan District covers South Jordan, Herriman, Riverton, and Bluffdale. Utah also has a strong charter school system.

Plan a scouting trip

Drive through neighborhoods at different times of day, visit parks and shopping centers, and test commute routes during rush hour. I-15 and Bangerter Highway are the main arteries.

Connect with a local realtor

A relocation specialist can share insights about neighborhoods, upcoming developments, and market conditions that you won't find in online listings alone.

Timing tip

Utah's real estate market heats up in spring (March–May) and cools in winter. If you're flexible, late fall and winter offer less competition and better negotiating power.

2

House Hunting & Logistics

8–4 weeks before your move

Start your home search

Set up MLS alerts with your realtor for your target suburbs and price range. Homes in SLC suburbs can move quickly in peak season — be ready to tour and make decisions fast.

Tour homes and explore neighborhoods

Check noise levels, traffic patterns, and proximity to TRAX light rail or FrontRunner commuter rail stations. Drive to nearby grocery stores, schools, and parks.

Make an offer and negotiate

Utah uses the REPC (Real Estate Purchase Contract). Understand the due diligence period, financing contingency, and appraisal contingency timelines.

Schedule a home inspection

Not required by Utah law, but strongly recommended. Pay special attention to foundation issues (clay-heavy soil can cause settling) and radon levels along the Wasatch Front.

Get moving quotes

Contact at least 3 movers. Long-distance moves fill up fast in summer — book early. If driving, note that mountain passes (I-80 through Wyoming) require caution in winter.

Research utility providers

Rocky Mountain Power (electricity), Dominion Energy (gas), your city's water department (varies by suburb), and internet (Comcast, Google Fiber, or CenturyLink — availability varies).

Notify your current landlord or list your home

Coordinate timelines so you're not carrying two mortgages or paying rent after you've moved.

Utah REPC tip

Utah's purchase contract has a "Due Diligence Deadline" — the date by which you must complete inspections and decide whether to proceed. Make sure you and your realtor track all contract deadlines carefully.

3

Closing & Prep

4–2 weeks before your move

Complete the closing process

In Utah, a title company typically handles closing and escrow. You'll sign documents, wire your down payment and closing costs, and receive the keys.

Set up Utah utilities for move-in day

Rocky Mountain Power (888-221-7070), Dominion Energy (800-323-5517), your city's water department, and schedule internet installation early — appointments book up.

Forward your mail

Submit a change of address with USPS at least 2 weeks before moving. Update banks, credit cards, insurance, and subscriptions.

Start packing non-essentials

Begin with seasonal items, books, and decorations. Label every box with its contents and destination room.

Transfer or obtain insurance

Set up homeowner's insurance before closing (required by your lender). Utah auto insurance minimums: 25/65/15 liability plus at least $3,000 PIP coverage.

Gather important documents

Birth certificates, Social Security cards, medical records, school transcripts, pet vaccination records, and vehicle titles. You'll need these for Utah residency.

DLD vs. DMV

Utah's Driver License Division (DLD) is separate from the Motor Vehicle Division (DMV). You'll visit a DLD office for your license and a DMV office for vehicle registration — plan for two trips. Book DLD appointments online at dld.utah.gov.

4

Final Countdown

2 weeks to moving day

Confirm moving company details

Reconfirm date, arrival window, and cost. Verify the delivery address and any access restrictions (gate codes, narrow streets, elevator reservations for condos).

Pack an essentials box

Toiletries, medications, chargers, change of clothes, basic tools, paper plates, snacks, and toilet paper. Keep this with you — don't put it on the truck.

Finish packing and deep clean

Complete packing, disassemble furniture, wrap fragile items. If leaving a rental, hire cleaners or do a thorough clean to get your deposit back.

Plan your travel route to Utah

From the east, I-80 through Wyoming is direct but has harsh winter weather. I-70 through Colorado to I-15 is a scenic alternative. SLC airport is a Delta hub with direct flights from most major cities.

Cancel or transfer local services

Gym memberships, cleaning services, pest control, lawn care, recurring subscriptions. Transfer prescriptions to a Utah pharmacy.

Prepare for Utah's climate

Utah is very dry (humidity often below 20%). Pack lip balm, lotion, and consider a humidifier. The SLC valley sits at 4,200–5,000 feet — you may feel mild altitude effects for the first few days. Drink extra water.

5

Getting Settled

First 2 weeks after your move

Unpack essentials first

Focus on kitchen, bedrooms, and bathrooms. Get beds set up and the kitchen functional before tackling the rest.

Get your Utah driver license

Visit a DLD office within 60 days. Bring your current license, proof of identity, Social Security number, and two proofs of Utah residency. Fee is approximately $52. Book appointments at dld.utah.gov.

Register your vehicle(s)

Within 60 days: get a VIN inspection, safety inspection, and emissions inspection (required in Salt Lake County). Bring your out-of-state title, insurance proof, and new Utah license.

Update your vehicle insurance

Update your policy to Utah. Minimum liability is 25/65/15 plus $3,000 PIP. Rates may change — compare quotes.

Register to vote

Register online at vote.utah.gov. Utah is a vote-by-mail state — your ballot will be mailed to your registered address.

Enroll children in school

Contact your neighborhood school or district office. You'll need proof of residency, immunization records (check immunize.utah.gov), birth certificate, and previous transcripts.

Find healthcare providers

Major systems: Intermountain Health (largest in Utah), University of Utah Health, and Steward Health Care. Primary Children's Hospital is nationally ranked for pediatrics.

Explore your new neighborhood

Find the closest Smith's, Harmons, WinCo, or Costco. Locate gas stations, pharmacies, and parks. Introduce yourself to neighbors — Utah communities are welcoming.

DLD wait times

Popular DLD locations like the Draper office can be booked weeks out. Book your appointment online at dld.utah.gov as soon as you arrive. The South Valley (Riverton) and West Valley offices sometimes have shorter waits.

6

Putting Down Roots

First 3 months

Get a UTA transit pass (if applicable)

TRAX light rail connects Sandy, Draper, and Murray to downtown SLC. FrontRunner runs Ogden to Provo. Monthly passes and FAREPAY cards available at rideuta.com.

Get outdoor recreation passes

Utah State Parks pass ($75/year), ski season passes (Ikon covers Brighton and Solitude), and familiarize yourself with Big and Little Cottonwood Canyon access.

Join community groups and activities

Facebook groups, Nextdoor, city recreation programs, youth sports leagues. Check your city's parks and recreation department for classes and events.

Register for city services

Trash/recycling schedules, park reservations, recreation center memberships, and library cards. Salt Lake County Library System serves many suburbs.

Learn Utah's liquor laws

Beer above 5% ABV, wine, and spirits are sold only at state DABC liquor stores. Grocery stores sell beer up to 5%. Liquor stores are closed Sundays and state holidays.

Prepare for Utah seasons

Get snow tires for canyon driving, buy a shovel and ice melt (most suburbs require clearing sidewalks within 24 hours of snowfall), and learn about summer wildfire smoke from western fires.

Start exploring Utah

Within an hour: 5 ski resorts, dozens of hiking trails, Antelope Island, Park City, downtown SLC. Utah's "Mighty Five" national parks are all within a day's drive.

Evaluate and optimize your commute

After a month, assess your route. I-15 peaks 7:00–8:30 AM and 4:30–6:30 PM. Consider Bangerter Highway, Mountain View Corridor, or Redwood Road as alternatives.
