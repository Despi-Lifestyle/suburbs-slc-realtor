How Utah stacks up

See how key cost categories in the SLC metro compare to the national average. Green means you're saving money.

Housing

At Average

Housing runs above the national median but significantly below comparable western metros like Denver ($590K), Portland ($530K), or anywhere in California.

Groceries

4% Lower

Groceries are slightly below average thanks to major retailers like WinCo, Costco, and local chains like Harmons and Smith's throughout the suburbs.

Utilities

8% Lower

Utah benefits from affordable natural gas and electricity. Expect higher heating bills in winter (Dec-Feb) and cooling costs in summer (Jul-Aug).

Transportation

5% Lower

Gas prices tend to be below average. TRAX light rail connects many south valley suburbs to downtown SLC. FrontRunner commuter rail reaches north.
