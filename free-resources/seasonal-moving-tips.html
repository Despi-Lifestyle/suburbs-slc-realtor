Seasonal Moving Tips for Utah

The best (and worst) times to buy and move in the SLC suburbs — and what each season means for your home search.

🌷

Spring (March - May)

Peak season begins
Best Selection

Spring is when the Utah real estate market comes alive. Inventory surges as sellers list their homes to catch the busy season. You'll have the most options to choose from, but you'll also face the most competition from other buyers. The weather turns pleasant, making it ideal for house hunting and touring neighborhoods.

Advantages

  • Highest inventory — most homes to choose from
  • Beautiful weather for touring neighborhoods
  • Time to close and move before school starts
  • Landscaping is visible (you can see what you're getting)

Watch Out For

  • Most competitive season — expect multiple offers
  • Prices tend to peak in May-June
  • Moving companies book up fast — reserve early
  • Allergy season (cottonwood fluff is real in Utah)
☀️

Summer (June - August)

Peak prices, peak activity
Best for Families

Summer is the most popular moving time for families with school-age kids. Prices are at their highest, but the advantage is clear: you can settle in before the school year starts. Utah summers are hot (90-100°F) but dry, with cool mountain evenings. Moving day will be warm — start early.

Advantages

  • Move in before school year starts
  • Long daylight hours for house hunting
  • Can fully experience outdoor lifestyle before buying
  • Good time to test commute routes (less school traffic)

Watch Out For

  • Highest prices of the year typically
  • Extreme heat during moving day (hydrate!)
  • Competition remains strong through July
  • Wildfire smoke can affect air quality some weeks
🍂

Fall (September - November)

Hidden opportunity season
Best Value

Fall is where savvy buyers find the best deals. Competition drops significantly after Labor Day as families settle into school routines. Sellers who didn't sell during summer get more motivated. Utah's fall foliage is spectacular — and you'll get a real sense of what the mountains look like in every season.

Advantages

  • Less competition, more negotiating power
  • Motivated sellers may accept lower offers
  • Beautiful weather (September-October is gorgeous)
  • Can close before holidays and enjoy your new home

Watch Out For

  • Less inventory — fewer homes to choose from
  • Days get shorter, less daylight for touring
  • Some new construction pauses before winter
  • School year disruption if you have kids
❄️

Winter (December - February)

The bargain hunter's season
Best Negotiation

Winter is the slowest season for Utah real estate, which means opportunity for patient buyers. Sellers listing in winter are usually motivated. You'll face virtually no competition. The downside: limited inventory, cold weather touring, and you can't see landscaping under the snow. But if value is your priority, this is your season.

Advantages

  • Lowest prices of the year on average
  • Almost no competition — you may be the only offer
  • Highly motivated sellers
  • You'll see how the home handles snow and cold

Watch Out For

  • Very limited inventory
  • Moving in snow is tough — hire professionals
  • Can't assess landscaping, drainage, or roof condition easily
  • Shorter days make scheduling tours harder

So when should YOU move?

The honest answer is: it depends on your priorities. If you want the most options, buy in spring. If you want the best deal, buy in fall or winter. If you have school-age kids, summer makes the most sense logistically. The "perfect" time is when your finances are ready and you've found the right suburb — the market adjusts, but finding the right neighborhood for your family is what really matters.
