First-Time Buyer Checklist for Utah

Utah-specific steps from pre-approval through closing day. Check items off as you go — your progress saves automatically.

1

Get Financially Ready

Before you even look at homes — 4-8 weeks

Check your credit score

Aim for 620+ minimum, 740+ for the best rates. Utah lenders offer competitive rates for strong credit.

Get pre-approved (not just pre-qualified)

In Utah's competitive market, sellers take pre-approval letters seriously. Shop 2-3 lenders for the best rate.

Research Utah down payment assistance programs

UHC (Utah Housing Corporation) offers first-time buyer programs with down payment help. Income limits apply.

Set your budget (including Utah property taxes)

Utah property taxes are relatively low (0.55-0.65% of home value) but factor in HOA fees, which are common in newer suburbs.

Utah-Specific: Down Payment Programs

Utah Housing Corporation (UHC) offers several programs for first-time buyers including the FirstHome Loan with competitive rates and down payment assistance of up to 6% of the loan amount. Income limits vary by county and household size. Natalie can connect you with lenders who specialize in these programs.

2

Find the Right Agent & Suburb

Critical step — especially as a relocator — 1-2 weeks

Choose an agent who knows SLC suburbs

A local expert will save you from common relocator mistakes — wrong suburb, wrong school boundary, overpaying.

Identify your top 2-3 suburbs

Don't try to search everywhere. Narrow to 2-3 areas based on budget, schools, commute, and lifestyle priorities.

Verify school boundaries for target neighborhoods

School boundaries don't always match city limits in Utah. Always confirm exact school assignments per address.

Research HOA rules if looking in newer communities

Many SLC suburb developments have HOAs. Fees range from $30-$200+/month. Review CC&Rs before buying.

3

House Hunting & Making Offers

The fun part — 2-8 weeks depending on market

Set up automated MLS alerts

Your agent should set these up. In competitive areas, you want to see new listings within hours, not days.

Tour homes (in-person or virtual if relocating from afar)

Video tours and FaceTime walkthroughs are common for out-of-state buyers. Your agent should offer this.

Make a competitive offer

Your agent will advise on offer strategy. In Utah, earnest money is typically 1-3% and shows seriousness.

Negotiate and go under contract

Utah uses the REPC (Real Estate Purchase Contract). Your agent will handle negotiations and paperwork.

4

Under Contract to Closing

The home stretch — typically 30-45 days

Schedule home inspection

Non-negotiable. Utah homes can have issues with foundation settling, radon, and water in basements. Budget $400-$600.

Get a radon test

Utah has higher-than-average radon levels. Testing costs ~$150 and mitigation (if needed) runs $800-$1,500.

Appraisal and title work

Your lender orders the appraisal. Title company handles the title search and prepares closing documents.

Final walkthrough

24-48 hours before closing. Verify repairs were made and the home is in agreed-upon condition.

5

Closing & Move-In

You're almost home!

Review closing disclosure (3 days before closing)

Compare to your loan estimate. Ask questions about anything that doesn't match.

Wire closing funds & sign documents

Utah typically closes at a title company. Bring your ID. Expect 1-2 hours of signing.

Get your keys and set up utilities

Rocky Mountain Power (electric), Dominion Energy (gas), and your local city for water/sewer/trash.

Update your address and get a Utah driver's license

Utah requires a new license within 60 days of establishing residency. Visit your local DLD office.
