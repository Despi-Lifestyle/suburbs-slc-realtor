The Neighborhood

South Jordan consistently ranks as one of the most desirable suburbs in the entire Salt Lake City metro area — and it's easy to see why. Nestled against the stunning backdrop of the Oquirrh Mountains to the west and the Wasatch Range to the east, this suburb offers the rare combination of breathtaking natural beauty with all the conveniences of modern suburban living.

Families are drawn here for the excellent schools, safe neighborhoods, and strong sense of community. The Daybreak community, one of the largest master-planned developments in Utah, is a standout — with its own lake, miles of trails, community pools, and a walkable town center. Whether you're looking for a brand-new build or an established neighborhood with mature trees, South Jordan has options at nearly every price point.

Why Families Love It

  • Excellent schools — Multiple highly-rated elementary, middle, and high schools. South Jordan is served by the Jordan School District, one of the top-performing districts in the state.
  • Mountain access — Big and Little Cottonwood Canyons are a short drive east, giving residents quick access to world-class skiing at Brighton, Solitude, Snowbird, and Alta.
  • Family infrastructure — Parks, rec centers, splash pads, and youth sports leagues are abundant. The community is built around families.
  • Daybreak community — If you want a walkable, modern, amenity-rich neighborhood, Daybreak is one of the most unique communities in all of Utah.
  • TRAX light rail — The South Jordan TRAX station provides direct rail service to downtown SLC, offering a car-free commute option.

Things to Consider

South Jordan's popularity means prices are on the higher end for the south valley. Commute times to downtown SLC can push 30+ minutes during rush hour, though the TRAX light rail extension helps. Some of the newer areas are still building out retail and dining, so depending on your specific neighborhood, you may need to drive a bit for restaurants and shopping.

Natalie's Insider Tip

"If you're considering South Jordan, check out Daybreak first — even if you don't end up buying there, it'll give you a great feel for what South Jordan living is all about. Also, don't overlook the area near the South Jordan TRAX station if commuting downtown matters to you."

The Neighborhood

Draper sits at the southeastern edge of the Salt Lake Valley, right where the suburbs end and the mountains begin. It's become one of Utah's most prestigious addresses, particularly among tech professionals, executives, and families who want premium homes with direct access to trails, canyons, and ski resorts.

The tech industry presence here is significant — companies like eBay, Overstock, and numerous startups have offices in the Draper/Point of the Mountain corridor, part of what locals call the "Silicon Slopes." This means high-paying jobs, growing infrastructure, and a community that skews younger, educated, and entrepreneurial.

Why Families Love It

  • Trail access from your doorstep — The Corner Canyon trail system has over 40 miles of hiking and mountain biking trails accessible from many neighborhoods.
  • Top-tier schools — Canyons School District consistently performs among the best in Utah. Corner Canyon High School is a destination school for relocating families.
  • Silicon Slopes proximity — If you work in tech, Draper puts you minutes from major employers without the commute to downtown SLC.
  • Upscale dining and shopping — Strong retail and restaurant scene, especially around the Traverse Mountain area.
  • Premium housing stock — Beautiful newer homes with mountain views, large lots, and high-end finishes.

Things to Consider

Draper is one of the more expensive suburbs on this list. Entry-level homes start higher than most neighboring communities, and premium neighborhoods can reach well into seven figures. The Bangerter Highway corridor can get congested during peak hours, and growth in the Point of the Mountain area means construction traffic is ongoing.

The Neighborhood

Herriman is the value play of the SLC suburbs. One of Utah's fastest-growing cities, it's exploded from a small farming town into a vibrant, modern suburb with brand-new master-planned communities, modern homes, and excellent value per square foot. If you want more house for your money — bigger lots, newer construction, more space — Herriman delivers.

The growth here is visible everywhere: new shopping centers, restaurants, parks, and schools are being built constantly. The Mountain View Corridor highway has dramatically improved commute access, and the energy of a community that's still forming its identity is infectious. Young families flock here because they can afford homes that would be out of reach in suburbs like Draper or Cottonwood Heights.

Why Families Love It

  • Best value in the south valley — At $575K median, Herriman offers the most home for the money among all 9 suburbs. New builds with modern finishes are attainable here.
  • Brand-new everything — Newer schools, parks, shopping, and community amenities. Much of the housing stock is less than 15 years old.
  • Growing restaurant and retail scene — Herriman Towne Center and new developments along the Mountain View Corridor are adding dining and shopping options quickly.
  • Space — Larger lots, more room between homes, and a less congested feel than the suburbs closer to the mountains.
  • Community events — Herriman City puts on excellent events including Herriman Howl (Halloween), the Fort Herriman Days festival, and summer concerts in the park.

Things to Consider

The trade-off for value is distance — Herriman is the farthest suburb from downtown SLC (35 minutes) and from the ski canyons (45+ minutes). The rapid growth means some areas feel like active construction zones. Walk Score is low at 13, so you'll need a car for virtually everything. Some established-neighborhood charm is traded for the new-build aesthetic.

Natalie's Insider Tip

"Herriman is where I send buyers who want the most bang for their buck. The south end near Butterfield Canyon offers larger lots with mountain views, while the newer developments near the Mountain View Corridor have the most convenient shopping access. If you're flexible on commute time, this is where your dollar stretches the farthest."

The Neighborhood

Sandy is the "best all-around" suburb on this list — and the data backs it up. It's centrally located, well-connected by transit, packed with dining and shopping, and sits right at the mouth of Little Cottonwood Canyon. It's the largest suburb by population and feels more like a small city than a bedroom community.

The Shops at South Town, a major regional mall, anchors the retail scene. Historic Sandy has a charming downtown area with local restaurants and boutiques. The east side near the canyons feels distinctly different from the west side — more mountain-oriented, with larger homes and quick ski access. Why Families Love It

  • Housing variety — From starter condos to luxury mountain estates, Sandy has the widest range of housing options.

Things to Consider

Sandy's popularity means homes move fast — the median days on market is just 10 days, making it one of the most competitive suburbs. Be prepared to act quickly. The I-15 corridor through Sandy gets congested during rush hours. The west side of Sandy (Jordan District) has a slightly different feel and lower price point than the east side (Canyons District).

The Neighborhood

If outdoor access is your number-one priority, Cottonwood Heights is the answer. Sitting right at the mouth of Big Cottonwood Canyon, this suburb offers the closest ski resort access of any neighborhood on this list — Brighton and Solitude are literally a 20-minute drive up the canyon. The Bonneville Shoreline Trail is practically in your backyard.

Cottonwood Heights is an established, tree-lined community with mid-century and newer homes set against a stunning mountain backdrop. It's smaller and quieter than Sandy or Draper, with a more residential feel. The community incorporated as its own city in 2005, giving residents local control over development. Brighton High School is a highly regarded campus in the Canyons District.

Why Families Love It

  • Unbeatable canyon access — 20 minutes to Brighton and Solitude ski resorts. During ski season, you can be on the slopes before the resort even fills up.
  • Hiking from your doorstep — The Bonneville Shoreline Trail, Big Cottonwood Canyon trails, and numerous neighborhood trailheads start right from the community.
  • High walkability — Walk Score of 41 is second only to Millcreek among these suburbs. The Cottonwood Heights Recreation Center and Cottonwood Mall area add amenities.
  • Established tree-lined streets — Mature neighborhoods with character, unlike the newer cookie-cutter developments in other suburbs.
  • Strong appreciation — Property values have been climbing steadily, with 25.7% YoY appreciation recently — a sign of strong, sustained demand.

Things to Consider

Cottonwood Heights is the most expensive suburb on this list at $856K median. The housing stock skews older — many homes are 1960s-1980s builds that may need updating. Inventory is very low, making it one of the hardest suburbs to buy into. During ski season, canyon traffic can back up into the neighborhood on powder days.

Natalie's Insider Tip

"Cottonwood Heights homes don't last long on the market. If you're targeting this suburb, you need to be pre-approved and ready to move fast. Also, look for homes that need cosmetic updates — you'll pay less per square foot for a home that needs new counters and paint but sits on a lot with million-dollar mountain views."

Incorporated as a city only in 2016, it's undergone a transformation from an unincorporated county area into a trendy, eclectic community that attracts young professionals, artists, and families who want proximity to downtown without actually living in the city.

The dining and nightlife scene along 3300 South and Highland Drive has exploded in recent years, with everything from craft breweries to upscale restaurants. Millcreek Canyon — a popular year-round outdoor destination — is a 10-minute drive from most neighborhoods. Millcreek is served by Granite School District, which has more variation in school quality than Canyons or Jordan. Research individual schools carefully. The housing stock is older — mostly mid-century homes — which means character but also potential renovation needs. Being close to downtown means more traffic and a slightly more urban feel than the south valley suburbs.

Chapter 13

School District Comparison

Four main school districts serve the nine suburbs in this guide. Which district you're in can significantly impact your home's value and your children's educational experience. Here's what you need to know.

Canyons School District

★ 8-9/10 · 33,000+ Students · 50+ Schools · Founded 2009

One of Utah's top-performing districts. Known for strong academic programs, excellent STEM offerings, and some of the best high schools in the state. Corner Canyon High School and Brighton High School are standout campuses. The district is newer (established 2009) which means modern facilities and innovative programs.

Serves: Draper, Sandy (east), Cottonwood Heights, Holladay (parts), Millcreek (parts)

Jordan School District

★ 7-9/10 · 55,000+ Students · 70+ Schools · Long-standing

The largest district in the south valley with a wide range of programs including dual-language immersion, IB programs, and strong extracurriculars. Performance varies by school, but the best schools compete with any in the state. South Jordan and Riverton schools particularly shine.

Serves: South Jordan, Riverton, Herriman, Bluffdale, Sandy (west)

Granite School District

★ 6-8/10 · 60,000+ Students · 80+ Schools · Utah's most diverse

Utah's most diverse school district serves a wide socioeconomic range. While overall ratings are slightly lower, individual schools in Millcreek and Cottonwood Heights areas rate well. Extensive language programs and strong special education services.

Serves: Millcreek (parts), Cottonwood Heights (parts), Holladay (parts)

Important: School Boundaries Don't Follow City Lines

School boundaries in the SLC suburbs can be tricky — a home on one side of a street might feed into a different school than the house across from it. Don't assume based on city alone. I always verify exact school assignments for every home I show to families. If schools are a top priority, let me know and I'll make sure we're only looking at homes in the right boundaries.

Charter Schools

Utah has a strong charter school system with open enrollment. If the assigned district school isn't ideal, there are often excellent charter options nearby. This is especially relevant in Granite District areas where charter schools can offer smaller class sizes and specialized programs.

Holladay & Millcreek — Close to Millcreek Canyon (hiking, biking, XC skiing). Big and Little Cottonwood Canyons 25-35 min. Walking-distance parks.

South Jordan — Daybreak has its own lake, trails, and pools. Ski resorts 35-40 min. Great for everyday family recreation.

Herriman, Riverton, Bluffdale — Wide-open spaces, foothill trails, equestrian access. Butterfield and Rose Canyon trails. Ski resorts 40-50 min but trade-off is more space.

Natalie's Outdoor Priority Guide

"If skiing is your #1 — choose Cottonwood Heights or Sandy. If hiking/biking is #1 — choose Draper or Cottonwood Heights. If you want a balance of outdoor access + value — choose Sandy. If you want land and open space — choose Bluffdale or Herriman. Every suburb has outdoor access, but the closest-to-canyon suburbs command a premium for a reason."

Your Relocation Timeline

Moving to a new state is a big project. Here's a week-by-week checklist to keep you on track from planning through settling in.

Phase 1: Research & Planning

12–8 weeks before your move
  • Define your family's priorities (commute, schools, budget, lifestyle)
  • Research the 9 SLC suburbs using this guide
  • Get your finances in order — pull credit, pay down debts
  • Get pre-approved for a mortgage (compare 2-3 lenders)
  • Research school districts if you have kids
  • Plan a scouting trip to drive neighborhoods
  • Connect with a local realtor (that's me!)

Phase 2: House Hunting & Logistics

8–4 weeks before your move
  • Start your active home search with MLS alerts
  • Tour homes and explore neighborhoods in person
  • Make an offer and negotiate (Utah uses the REPC contract)
  • Schedule home inspection — check foundation and radon
  • Get moving quotes from 3+ companies
  • Research utility providers (Rocky Mountain Power, Dominion Energy)
  • Notify your current landlord or list your home

Phase 3: Closing & Prep

4–2 weeks before your move
  • Complete the closing process at a Utah title company
  • Set up Utah utilities for move-in day
  • Forward your mail (USPS change of address)
  • Start packing non-essentials
  • Transfer or obtain homeowner's and auto insurance
  • Gather important documents for Utah residency

Phase 4: Move & Settle In

Moving day through first 3 months
  • Get your Utah driver license at a DLD office (within 60 days)
  • Register your vehicles (VIN, safety, emissions inspections required)
  • Enroll children in school
  • Register to vote at vote.utah.gov
  • Find healthcare providers (Intermountain Health, U of U Health)
  • Get outdoor recreation passes (state parks, ski passes)
  • Join community groups and neighborhood activities
  • Learn Utah's liquor laws (state DABC stores for spirits/wine)

Utah-Specific Tip: DLD vs. DMV

Utah's Driver License Division (DLD) is separate from the Motor Vehicle Division (DMV). You'll visit a DLD office for your license and a DMV office for vehicle registration — plan for two trips. Book your DLD appointment online at dld.utah.gov as soon as you arrive, as popular locations book weeks out.
